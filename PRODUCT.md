# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users
Primary users are JKT48 fans tracking exclusive event availability for themselves during active sales windows. They check repeatedly from mobile and desktop while inventory is moving quickly, and they need to spot which members, sessions, and purchase paths are still actionable without parsing a noisy upstream flow. A secondary use is portfolio review: people evaluating the project as a demonstration of practical frontend craft and resilient data handling.

## Product Purpose
GLOBAL EXCLUSIVE MONITOR exists to turn fast-moving JKT48 exclusive sale data into a clearer and more dependable monitoring experience. It sits between users and an unstable upstream flow, reorganizing sessions, member availability, and ticket movement into a view that is faster to scan and more resilient when the source service is stale, queued, or inconsistent. Success means users can understand what is still available at a glance, recover quickly when upstream conditions degrade, and act with less friction than they could on the source platform alone.

## Positioning
The fastest way to scan live JKT48 exclusive availability, even when the upstream flow gets noisy.

## Brand Personality
Fast, clear, resilient. The interface should feel immediate and sharp, but never frantic. It should stay calm under pressure, make change visible without theatrics, and signal trust through operational clarity rather than decorative polish.

## Anti-references
Do not let this drift into a generic crypto or trading dashboard with vanity metrics, neon drama, or speculative-dashboard theatrics. It should also avoid reading like a cute fandom scrapbook, a sparse marketing landing page, or a heavy enterprise admin panel. The product needs to stay direct, task-first, and operational.

## Design Principles
- Put scan speed ahead of decoration.
- Treat queue, stale, and failure states as first-class product moments.
- Keep the next useful action obvious.
- Make dense information feel controlled, not cramped.
- Preserve trust with clear semantics, strong contrast, and resilient feedback.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG AA baseline. Contrast must stay strong in both themes, including placeholders, labels, and status surfaces. Interactive controls should remain touch-friendly under pressure, reduced motion must be respected, and status meaning must never depend on color alone.
